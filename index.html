Paliament SDG

Tracking SDG-related activity in national parliaments

We are building an innovative platform to track national parliaments activity related to the Sustainable Development Goals and offer this information openly and freely through an online search tool for users to search, download and share.

This tool will overcome the challenges presented by the cross-cutting nature of the Agenda 2030 for the implementation, evaluation and monitoring of the SDGs.

It will be an adaptation of TiPi, an existing online tool implemented in Spain since 2015, which monitors all parliamentarian activity in 21 selected topics related with the fight against poverty and inequality

How we will do it?

We will gather all information published by the national parliament into a database and, through an automatic process of massive-tagging, it will be classified according to its linkage to specific SDG goals of targets.

Through an intuitive, attractive and accesible interface, all relevant information will be provided. Even the activity which might be less obvious at first sight: Which SDGs are affected by a certain legislative initiative? Whichi of them receive the most attention by MPs, and which are being left behind? Which MPs are most active in the implementation of the 2030 Agenda, and who is neglecting it?

Who we are?

CIECODE is a Spanish think-and-do tank specialized in policy coherence for sustainable development. Our mission is to promote an informed and fact-based public debate on sustainable development.

We develop innovative technologies to automatically track sustainable development-related activity and offer public, direct, and free access to this information to citizens and key stakeholders.

Where does this come from?

CIECODE’s new platform to track SDG activity is an adaptation to the Agenda 2030 framework of TiPi (which stands for Transparency + Information + Participation + Influence), another CIECODE’s innovative project that generates open-data and promotes transparency, access to information, and political accountability by allowing the monitoring of all parliamentarian activity related to the fight against poverty and inequality.

TiPi is able to automatically classify the parliamentarian activity in 21 strategically selected topics, through the an advanced scraping and labeling technology.

Why parliaments?

National parliaments have a key role in the SDG implementation process. They are the most relevant and best-positioned public institutions to ensure coherence with sustainable development in the hundreds of thousands of laws, legislative initiatives, public plans and budgets that will be passed in each country in the years to come.

We are now adapting the technology to the Spanish Parliament, but it could also be expanded at a subnational level or outside Spain (that is what we are aiming for!). Whether it is a third-country in another continent, a German länder or the EU Parliament who shows interest, TiPi could be quickly implemented in a short period of time due the open source and code used.
